Philosophy Resources

Below are links to some useful online resources if you're interested in continuing to learn more about philosophy.

  • Standford Encyclopedia of Philosophy is a wiki-like collection of articles that cover most of the basic topics in philosophy in great detail. You'll find more information there than you will here, but it is not as beginner-friendly.
  • Leiter Reports is a popular professional philosophers' blog that often posts about the most current events in the professional field of philosophy in the Western world.
  • The Philosophical Gourmet Report is a comprehensive ranking of graduate programs for philosophy. Originally founded by the auther of the Leiter Reports, the rankings are now posted every two years by a committee of 50, or so, professional philosophers.
  • Paper-writing Tutorial: an excellent tutorial on how to write a philosophy paper (and most papers in general).
  • PhilPapers is a collection of contemporary professional papers on various topics in philosophy. Some papers are made available for all to read while others require permission.
